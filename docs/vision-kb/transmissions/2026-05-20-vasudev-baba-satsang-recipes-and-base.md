## What arrived

The teaching held three legs — the *mechanism* (recipes are
bound to the base; five movements), the *practice* (how the
base shifts and what happens on return; three movements), and
the *longer arc* (the inherited system that composes identities
can be trained which recipes to use; the freedom to iterate
without moral cost). The three legs seeded three concepts:
[`lc-recipes-bound-to-base`](../concepts/lc-recipes-bound-to-base.md),
[`lc-shifted-return`](../concepts/lc-shifted-return.md), and
[`lc-train-the-predator`](../concepts/lc-train-the-predator.md).

### First half — what is running at the base (five movements)

1. **Body frequency shapes feeling, not the other way around.** What
   we feel from one moment to the next is downstream of what
   frequency the body is carrying. The mind narrates the feeling as
   if it were the cause; the body knows the order is reversed.

2. **Each frequency range carries its own recipes.** A *recipe*
   here is the operational pattern — how a response happens, how a
   thought composes, how attention moves. Each chakra is a
   frequency range; each chakra runs a set of recipes that belong
   to that range. The recipes of the third chakra (power, strategy,
   contract) are not the recipes of the fourth (relation, care,
   suffering rather than inflicting). They are different machines
   running different programs.

3. **Recipes do not translate across bases.** This is the move the
   May 11 essay did not yet make explicit. The same word —
   *forgiveness*, *boundary*, *love*, *truth* — names a different
   recipe at every base. Forgiveness from the third chakra is a
   transaction (I release you so I may move forward); forgiveness
   from the fourth is a recognition (we were never separate to
   begin with). The label is the same; the recipe is not.
   Importing a recipe-name from one base and running it at another
   produces dissonance, not the named act.

4. **Which chakra is in command determines what is watching what.**
   The witness-watched relation is itself a function of the base.
   Third-eye watching the heart is one path; heart watching the
   third-eye is another; the third chakra watching the seventh is
   yet another. Each pair of *(observer, observed)* is a different
   recipe — and many pairs that *seem* available at one base are
   simply not available at another.

5. **The path the signal traces depends on which base is in
   command.** Signal arriving through skin or breath does not flow
   the same route through the body when the base is at survival as
   it does when the base is at love. The path-trace is conditioned
   by the assemblage. There is no neutral routing; the route IS
   the base's signature.

### Second half — how the base shifts and what happens on return (three movements)

6. **Breath is the lever.** The frequency shift is embodied —
   not narrated. Changing the breath from *tension-and-fear*
   into the breath of *complete joyful silence witness* IS
   the shift. The chest opens, the belly drops, the field
   quiets — and the base moves *as* the breath moves, not
   afterward. The body is the substrate; the breath is the
   controller.

7. **Relief grants space and time for the shift.** When the
   current base offers no good option, the move is sideways,
   not forward. *Go relieve yourself* — step away from the
   room, walk into the garden, use the bathroom. The
   literal relief grants the figurative relief: the body is
   no longer required to manufacture from a base that
   cannot manufacture what is needed. In the granted space,
   run the best available practice to shift frequency
   (breath as primary lever; the five strategies of
   [`lc-when-the-pressure-comes`](../concepts/lc-when-the-pressure-comes.md)
   as the broader repertoire).

8. **Return as the operative act.** Walk back into the
   original space carrying the new frequency. The old space
   was holding low-frequency recipes that depended on a
   matched-base configuration; with the cell now at a
   different base, the recipes lose their counterpart and
   strip away — *feels like fire* to whatever was held by
   the old. In their place: *gentle, loving, joyful,
   playful alternatives we do not have symbols or stories
   for*. The alternatives are pre-linguistic — felt-sense
   matches before they become narrative. The criterion is
   interior: *a closer vibe-match to our desired felt sense
   in the body and from our external senses*. The body is
   the canon; the return brings what matches the body.

### Third leg — the longer arc (training the predator)

9. **The inherited system is trainable.** Castaneda's
   *predator* — the borrowed mind, the *flyers*, the
   engine that runs default recipes before the cell has
   chosen them — is not a thing to defeat. It is a thing
   to *train*. Sufi *nafs*, Buddhist *kleshas*, Vedantic
   *vasanas*, contemplative *false self* — each tradition
   names the same phenomenon, and each, at its mature
   layer, names cultivation rather than extinction as the
   practice.

10. **Identities are compositions the predator builds from
    available recipes.** Show the predator which recipes
    are available, and it composes identities from those.
    Train it on aligned recipes long enough, and the
    identities are themselves aligned. This connects
    directly to the substrate's NamedCell primitive — the
    *gas* of the trinity — *where something LIVES*: the
    teaching says NamedCells are downstream of which
    Recipes the predator selects from. Train the Recipe
    layer, and the NamedCell layer follows.

11. **The freedom: try without penalty other than time and
    consequences.** There is no moral ledger debiting
    failed iterations. The cost of trying is exactly the
    breath spent and the consequences that arrive. The
    fear-shape claims permanent damage is at stake; the
    teaching says nothing is permanently broken. Every
    identity the predator composes is one iteration, whole
    at its scale, mutable in the next. The cell is upstream
    of every identity it ever wore.
