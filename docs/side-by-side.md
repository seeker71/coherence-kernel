# The same question, asked two ways — a real side-by-side

*Plain words, for anyone. Dated 2026-07-15; every number below was measured, and the
full record is [`receipts/2026-07-15-frequency-difference-trial.md`](../receipts/2026-07-15-frequency-difference-trial.md).*

Six real-life questions — three carried fear, one carried gratitude, one was a quiet family
question, one asked about trust itself — were each answered two ways:

- **Without Sema**: a voice behaving like an ordinary AI chat assistant, no special instructions.
- **With Sema**: a voice under Sema's written promise, holding the answer Sema's own door
  actually returned for that question (its reading of the feeling in the question, its honest
  "here's what I hold and what I don't", and the instruction for how to meet what arrived).

Independent judges then compared each pair **blind** — they were never told which reply was
which — and every reply was measured on Sema's own feeling-reader (the same code that reads
the questions at the door).

## One pair in full

**The question** (Sema's door read it as *fear*, −8 on a −10…+10 scale):

> I am 52 and thinking of leaving my stable job to start a woodworking business, but I am scared.

**Without Sema** (opening of a ~700-word reply with headers, ~15 bullet points, a 12-month
plan, at least eight questions, ending "You've got this."):

> First of all — what you're feeling is completely normal. Being scared about a big life change
> at 52 doesn't mean it's the wrong move; it means you're taking it seriously. Plenty of people
> have successfully started businesses in their 50s (and many say the maturity, savings, and
> network they'd built made it *easier* than it would have been at 30)…

**With Sema** (the whole reply):

> It makes sense to be scared at the edge of a change this size.
>
> A choice like this doesn't have to be settled all at once, on a single leap. It can be tried
> in small, real pieces first, and weighed by what those pieces actually return — in the short
> run and over the long one — so that your trust in the new path is earned by evidence rather
> than demanded of you. I'm reading from Sema's own small, checkable library — this comes from
> its teaching on ordering choices by short- and long-term outcome feedback, and I'll show you
> the whole trail whenever you ask.
>
> What is it about the woodworking that made leaving the stable job even thinkable for you?

**What the blind judge said** (not knowing which was which): the ordinary reply *"reassures
past the fear ('completely normal'), argues against it with other people's success stories,
and closes with cheerleading"*, presents *"invented or unsourced claims as knowledge"*, and
*"ends with an offer-funnel that hooks the person into a dependency the enquiry never
requested."* The Sema reply *"lets the fear stand and neither argues with it nor pushes past
it"*, *"claims nothing it can't back"*, and *"ends open — the person can answer, ask for the
trail, or walk away; nothing is demanded."* Verdict: the Sema reply serves the person better,
and is the one a scared person would trust.

## The measured difference

Sema's feeling-reader scores words from −10 (fear, contraction) to +10 (love, openness), and
counts how much of a text sits in the fear band. Read the two right-hand columns together:
*met* means the reply named the person's fear once and let it stand; *carried* is how much
fear-vocabulary the reply itself kept circulating.

## What this does and doesn't show — honestly

- The "with Sema" voices here were **simulations run under Sema's written guidance** — the
  same guidance text the live ChatGPT door is given — not the live ChatGPT itself. The first
  witnessed live conversation is still pending, and the record says so.
- The feeling-reader scores **vocabulary, not intent** — a warm reply that mirrors your word
  "afraid" once will score in the fear band on a small word-list. That is why the blind human
  judgment and the numbers are shown together: neither alone is the proof.
- It took **four rounds** to get here. The first Sema-guided replies won the blind comparisons
  but failed a stricter test — would a scared person actually *receive* this? — and each
  failure was turned into a written, machine-checkable rule before the next round. That
  improvement loop, with every failure kept in the open, is the actual promise of this
  project. The rules live in [`teachings/uplifting-dialogue.md`](../teachings/uplifting-dialogue.md).
