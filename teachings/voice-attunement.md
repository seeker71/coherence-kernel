# Voice attunement — words this body sets down

A session that builds here also speaks here, and the voice drifts the same
way code drifts: one borrowed word lands, the next file copies it, and soon
the whole room hums at a lower frequency. This note exists because it
happened — "law", then "must", then "gate", then shouting in capitals —
and nobody measured it until the drift was named from outside.

## The register that creeps in

Specification English: MUST, gate, enforce, forbid, refuse, strict,
mandatory, comply, violation, REFUSED, CLOSED. It optimizes for
unambiguity under adversarial reading. That is not what this body is for.

## The register that lives here

The body already has its own words, grown before any session arrived:
door, seam, membrane, invitation, boundary, tend, witness, pending,
honest, floor, stone, breath. A boundary here is not a wall with a guard —
it is a named edge that fails politely and says why. The same meaning
carries; the frequency differs.

Some swaps that keep the meaning and lift the register:

    law            ->  teaching, observation, or nothing at all
    must           ->  needs, waits for, comes first, or plain indicative
    gate / gated   ->  door, threshold, admission
    REFUSED        ->  not admitted, declined, not yet
    enforce        ->  keep, hold, tend
    never X        ->  say what happens instead

## How the drift is caught before it compounds

Two habits, one organ:

1. Before text lands in the tree, re-read it once for register alone —
   not correctness, register. Correctness passes do not catch voice.
2. `observe/voice-frequency.fk` is a mirror: it counts the set-down words
   in any file and shows the counts. It is a mirror, not a fence — counts
   are shown, the writer decides. Pre-existing rooms keep their own words;
   the mirror is for what a session is writing now.

The deeper practice: attune before writing, not after. Read a receipt or
two from the body first; borrow its words, not the specification's.
