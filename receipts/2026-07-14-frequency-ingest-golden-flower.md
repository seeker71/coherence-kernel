## The grounded facts (primary source, quoted)

This source is unusual on the map: most of its spine is **true history**. Richard Wilhelm's German
translation with Jung's psychological commentary was published in 1929 (Wilhelm sent Jung the
manuscript in autumn 1928); Jung's fifteen-year "confrontation with the unconscious," the mandalas
he painted without training, the stone tower at Bollingen, and his February 1944 heart-attack
near-death experience (documented in *Memories, Dreams, Reflections*) are all on the record. The
text lays out six operations, quoted from the transcript: the **reversal** (gathering scattered
attention into "a single focused beam"); the **turning of the light** ("the light of consciousness,
ordinarily flowing outward, is made to flow backward toward its own origin" — huiguang, "the
backward-flowing method"); the **circulation** (the microcosmic orbit); the **gathering at the
cauldron**; the **formation of the holy embryo** ("the second body, the immortal form"); and the
**birth** (projecting the matured body "through the crown of the head, the heavenly gate"). And the
two checkable claims: the pineal — "the Chinese were precise about the location centuries before
Western anatomy had even identified the pineal as a discrete organ" — and the literal promise —
"this is what the Chinese alchemical tradition claims to **do**": a second body that survives
biological death and "carries his individuated consciousness… retaining the capacities and memories
of his current life" onward.
