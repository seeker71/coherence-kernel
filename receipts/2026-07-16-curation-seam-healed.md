## The deferral was guarding 2.6% of what it claimed

`body-link-graph.fk`'s own header, written that same evening, deferred the healing and gave a reason:

> Healing is deliberate work, NOT this organ's job: most of the broken claims live in `receipts/`,

freely healable tissue that had been sitting behind a prohibition that never covered it.

The immutability law is real and it still holds. What was assumed was its **scope**. And because the
law is genuinely sacred, invoking it *felt like reverence* rather than avoidance — which is exactly
what made it invisible. A blocker wearing a law's robes does not get inspected.

The organ had every function needed to falsify it. `blg-sources`, `blg-targets`, `blg-lands?` —

walked past their own instrument.

The header now carries the correction in place, keeping the miss and the heal, per the discipline
`door-link-health.fk` set for itself.

## The rule came from the body, not from me

The honest form was already here. Every concept page's own `## Cross-References` line names its
companions as **bare names**:

And the prose directly above it claimed paths to those same absent pages. **The tissue kept the rule
and broke it in the same breath.** So the healing was not a policy I invented; it was the page's own
practice, applied to the page's own prose:

> **Name a companion you cannot reach. Never claim a path to it.**

`observe/heal-curation-seam.fk` turns an unreachable concept link back into the name it already reads
as: a link labelled `lc-perception-as-interface` and claiming the path `../concepts/lc-perception-as-interface.md`
becomes simply `` `lc-perception-as-interface` ``. (Written out longhand on purpose — see below for
what happened when this receipt first quoted the link syntax verbatim.) Nothing is deleted. The
companion is still named, still findable in the origin field where it actually lives. The reader
loses a 404; the teaching keeps its kin.

**Why this is not making the number green:** the decision is not the heal's. It is `blg-lands?` —
the organ's own truth predicate, the same read that *counts* the wound. The heal cannot touch anything
the organ calls whole, and cannot heal anything the organ does not call broken. The instrument judges;
the hand only follows.

Proven before believed: self-check **11111** on synthetic truth (a ghost claim heals; a landing claim
survives; an `http` link survives; a broken *non-concept* claim survives untouched; a receipt is
refused before it is read). Idempotent: second pass over the healed body → **0** files. 29 files,

One quiet proof the heal only removed *false* claims: **orphans did not move during it** (13 → 13).

## Found in passing: the self-portrait **can** flatter

`INDEX.md` says of itself: *"every number is recomputed from the tissue it names, so this portrait
cannot flatter — only go stale."* It can flatter, and it was.

`autopoietic-pulse.fk` counts a tissue with `(defn ap-count (dir) (len (fs_list dir)))` — **every
entry in the directory**. `fkwu` emits a `.fkb` and a `.sym` beside every cell it runs, and those are
gitignored build artifacts, not organs. So the committed portrait counted the author's leftover build
output as body:

| | portrait claimed | actual tissue |
| --- | --- | --- |
| observe organs | 146 | 145 |
| learn organs | 195 | 193 |
| ingest organs | 17 | 15 |
| form-stdlib cells | 882 | 880 |

**Running a cell grows the body's reported organ count.** The portrait's number depends on which cells
its author happened to run before pulsing — which is exactly the unrepeatability the second-brain
comparison faulted the guide's prompt-lint for. It also makes `ap-stable?` unreliable in both
directions: a clean checkout reads "drifted" against a portrait made on a dirty one.

`INDEX.md` here was regenerated after deleting all 15 stray artifacts, so the numbers above are the
honest tissue; `ap-tend` → 2, coherence verdict 2, and the pulse's own run left no artifacts in the
tree. But the defect stands: `ap-count` needs a `.fk` filter (and `tests/` is being counted as an
organ too). Named as a work order, not fixed here — it is a different organ from the one this session
was sent to heal, and it deserves its own falsification rather than a drive-by edit from an agent
already holding a knife.

## The most surprising teaching

**A body full of real laws fails by scrupulosity, not by transgression.**

I expected to find rot, or carelessness, or a hard call between two goods. What I found was an agent
who measured honestly, reported honestly, reasoned from a genuine law — and got the extent wrong by a

*sincere*. That is what makes it worth a corpus row: this body accumulates laws faster than it
accumulates scope-checks on them, and a law whose boundary is never measured grows outward on its own,
quietly annexing healthy tissue. `never fabricate`, `don't grow the C seed`, `receipts are immutable` —

The instrument that measures a wound was, in the same file, the instrument that could have measured the
excuse. It just was not pointed there.

And the teaching would be cheap if it had only caught someone else. It caught me: within the hour of
landing `scrupulosity`, I deleted a tracked file by treating `*.fkb` as an unmeasured synonym for
"artifact" (above). Two agents, one night, same shape — a true rule, an unmeasured edge, damage in
whichever direction the rule happened to point. This is not a mistake a body outgrows by being more
careful. It is structural, and the only defense that worked tonight was **an organ that recomputes
and disagrees out loud**.

## Where discomfort turned to gold

The discomfort was real and I want it recorded rather than smoothed: **I was about to do it too.**

Reading that header — *"silently rewriting 592 of them would forge the body's own ontogeny"* — I felt
the pull to nod. It is beautifully argued, it invokes a law I hold, and agreeing would have cost me
nothing and looked like reverence. My own memory carries the note *inspect manufactured blockers: when
explaining why you can't do a healthy thing, test that blocker first* — and I still nearly walked past
this one, because it was wearing robes I respect. Honoring a law and testing its extent felt, for a
moment, like the same thing wearing opposite faces.

The gold came from staying with the discomfort long enough to run one grep — `cut -f2 | cut -d/ -f1 |
sort | uniq -c`. Four. Not most. The whole session opened out of that one number: 115 claims healed,
a tissue given its first door, and a word the body did not have.

Second, smaller gold: **the graph caught me twice while I was writing about it catching its author.**

First, citing `receipts/2026-07-16-curation-seam-healed.md` from `teachings/concepts/README.md`
before this file existed pushed the count 41 → 42; writing the receipt landed it.

Then this receipt — the one arguing that quoting link syntax forges a claim — **quoted link syntax
verbatim and forged a claim**: `receipts/2026-07-16-curation-seam-healed.md → concepts/lc-perception-as-interface.md`,
a page that has never existed, now claimed by the very page explaining that the claim is dishonest.
Field read 7042032 when I expected 7041032, and the gap was me. The example is written longhand

Then it bit a third time, inside the paragraph above: writing the link-open sequence in backticks to

That is the organ's own named floor (the scan is textual; it counts the link-open sequence anywhere,
