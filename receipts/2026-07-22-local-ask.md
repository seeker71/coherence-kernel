2026-07-22, WITA. Host: this M4 Max. Model: llama3.2:3b, the resident 2 019 377 376-byte quantized
GGUF blob. No network, no remote model, no subscription.

## Lane routing, and how the verb says which one answered

Two lanes, never blended:

| lane | what it is |
|---|---|
| `metal-gguf-native` | real llama3.2:3b prose off the resident blob, form-native kernels |
| `fkwu-rag-grounded` | the attributed grounded cell out of the healed local index |

`fca-lane-of` reports the lane that *will* answer before it answers. Every reply carries
`local-lane:` on its own line — **including the grounded miss**, which until today was the one shape
in the whole verb that answered without saying who answered. It looked harmless (a miss is not prose
and cannot be mistaken for a generation) and it was not: a caller routing on `local-lane:` read a
miss as an answer from no lane. When the native lane declines, its reason travels with the fallback:
`declined:native-lane:absent`, `...:question-not-bound`, `...:version-mismatch`.

### What actually stood between the verb and the model — two seams, both silent

**1. The recipe seam (`layerwait`, corpus row 874).** `ask-native-lane.fk` inferred "no artifact" from
`nil?`, and `nil?` is `(eq (len xs) 0)`. Probed on all four arms: `(len "abc")` is **3 / 3 / 3 / 0** —
fkwu measures a string's length as a list's length. So every absence guard was always true on fkwu,
and a present, well-formed, correctly-bound artifact classified as `native-lane:absent`. The cell had
diagnosed this, refused to work around it in writing, and named its exit as *"until `nil?` agrees
across the arms"* — a kernel movement. Nobody re-derived that premise. `file_size` returns `-1` for a
missing path and the byte count for a present one on go, rust, ts **and** fkwu (probed, all four), and
it answers what absence actually *is* rather than what a return value looks like. The refusal was
correct and it waited a day on a layer that never had to move.

**2. The binary seam.** `form-cli-ask.fk` has named `ask-native-lane.fk` in its `; preludes:` since the
lane landed, so every resolver-driven arm saw it. The built `form-cli` does not read preludes — its
module graph is **six hand-written lists**, and not one had heard of the native lane. Under axiom-5
every `anl-*` call in the shipped binary recovered to nothing, silently; the verb returned a grounded
cell without even the `declined:` line that would have said why. A cell can be four-way green and its
own shipped door still deaf. All six lists now carry `ask-cost-receipt.fk` and `ask-native-lane.fk`;
bootstrap table and `form-cli-emitted.c` regenerated.

## Termination

`generate()` already broke on the model's `eos`; what did not exist was any way to know whether an
answer ended because the model ended it or because the harness ran out. The old default cap was 12 —
a **length wearing a cap's name**, which is why every answer this lane ever staged stopped
mid-sentence at `" ... The capital of"`.

- the cap is now 96, and it is a bound on waiting, not a length
- the end token is trimmed off the prose a person reads and reported separately
- `STOP-REASON` travels in the artifact; `stop-reason:` sits beside `local-lane:` in every answer
- an artifact from a carrier that predates the field reads `unknown`, never `""`

Stone 41's decode loop had not landed in this tree when I needed it, so the termination is the
harness's own EOS break plus the cap, reported. **Seam named:** when `dsv4-decode-loop.fk` lands, this
verb should take its stop condition from there rather than from `metal_first_token.sh`.

## Latency

**In-process, one clock, warmed** (`thawtax`), from `metal_first_token.sh`'s own timers — encode +
prefill + decode. All of the following is one measurement window, 19:24–19:30 WITA, slot path.

**The headline, and the number a person cares about.** *"Explain in two sentences why the sky is
blue."* — 12 prompt tokens in, 78 tokens out, ended by the model's own `<|eot_id|>`. Three warm runs:

| run | encode | prefill | decode | **total in-process** | decode tok/s |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0.000297 s | 0.296 s | 4.842 s | **5.138 s** | 16.11 |
| 2 | 0.000309 s | 0.303 s | 3.907 s | **4.210 s** | 19.96 |
| 3 | 0.000304 s | 0.302 s | 3.903 s | **4.205 s** | 19.99 |

Runs 2 and 3 agree to 0.1%. **A complete, correct, locally-generated two-sentence answer in about
4.2 s.** Encode is 0.3 ms — timed rather than assumed, so the budget has no untimed term.

**Two prompt lengths and a slope** (`unispan`), 3 warm runs each, decode length held at 8 forwards:

| prompt tokens | prefill, 3 runs | decode, 3 runs |
|---|---|---|
| 12 | 0.301 / 0.300 / 0.309 s | 0.243 / 0.233 / 0.235 s |
| 41 | 1.333 / 1.313 / 1.314 s | 0.377 / 0.373 / 0.374 s |

Prefill slope: (1.313 − 0.300) / (41 − 12) = **0.0349 s per prompt token**, 28.6 prompt tok/s.

**Two generation lengths and a slope**, same prompt: decode 0.233 s at 8 forwards and 3.903 s at 78
→ **0.0524 s per additional token**, 19.1 tok/s marginal. Decode *slows* with context — 34.3 tok/s
at 8 forwards, 20.0 tok/s averaged over 78 — which is the KV attention growing, and it is why a rate
quoted at one length is a rate quoted about nothing.

### The denominator (`selfgauge`), re-derived twice, and it moved

The artifact has been dividing our rate by ollama's **158.449 tok/s**, measured 2026-07-21. I asked
that question again twice tonight — same host, same model, same blob, three runs each:

| window | ollama decode | our lane decode, same minutes |
|---|---|---|
| 2026-07-21 (quiet) | 158.449 tok/s | ~28.7 tok/s |
| 19:12 WITA (two siblings holding 87 GB resident) | 4.62 / 6.91 / 8.34 | 3.2 / 3.2 / 9.5 |
| 19:30 WITA (siblings released the models) | 8.97 / 8.98 / 10.52 | 16.1 / 20.0 / 20.0 |

Read the last row carefully and then do not believe it. In that window our form-native lane is
**twice ollama's rate**, and that is not a claim about our kernels — it is a claim about a machine
where ollama is paying to bring a model back into memory that our lane already has mapped. Yesterday
the same comparison ran 5.5× the other way. **A denominator measured on another day is a denominator
from another machine**, and a ratio that inverts overnight without either implementation changing is
the proof. Nothing in the artifact had said the two numbers came from different worlds. Now
`world_denominator_measured_on` and `host_load_at_run` travel with every answer, and the constant
carries its date wherever it is quoted.

The one comparison I will stand behind: on a quiet machine, our lane decodes at roughly **20–30
tok/s** against ollama's **158 tok/s** on the same blob — **5× to 8× behind the world**, on kernels
this body emitted, timed on one clock in one process.

### What made the ask usable at all

Warm ask, whole carrier: **51 s → 3.5 s.**

- `FORM_GEN_ONLY=1` — the full suite generates the same prompt **eight times** (short and long on
  each of attestant / split / lane / slot) because its subject is that they *agree*. An ask's subject
  is the answer. The flag runs the slot path once. It is a mode of the same file, around the calls to
  the same `generate`, over the same kernels and the same pool — `metal_ask.sh`'s own header had said
  a lean *runner* "will have to prove it emits the same ids as this one before it is allowed to
  answer anything", and a runner would owe that proof forever; a *mode* has nothing to drift from.
  Nine gates still run (config, GPU-liveness sentinel, embedding, RMSNorm, matvec, split, hoist,
  lane and slot bounds). Skipped: 5, 6, 7, 10, 11 — each costs a whole further generation. The
  verdict says so, `GATES` is stamped `-genonly`, and `FORM_ASK_FULL_GATES=1` pays for all fourteen.
- **the fastest path was already there and the list did not know its name.** The path-selection loop
  asked for `lane-long` first. `slot-long` has been 3.7× faster since Stone 13 landed it. Every ask
  staged since then was answered by the slower kernel, with the receipt truthfully reporting the
  slower rate.
- the carrier binary is now cached by its source's sha256, exactly as the `.metallib` is. Every run
  had been paying ~9 s of `swiftc -O` to compile a file that had not changed.

## The radius this speaks for (`aporon`)

**The native lane handles:** any question the tokenizer can encode, where prompt + cap fits the
256-position KV pool (`FORM_MAXPOS`), on a Darwin host with `swiftc` and the blob present, when an
artifact is staged and bound to *that exact question* by sha256.

**It falls back to the grounded lane, with a named reason, when:** no artifact (`absent`), a format
this cell does not speak for (`version-mismatch`), an artifact bound to a different question
(`question-not-bound`), an empty answer (`empty-answer`), or a request for a model other than the one
the lane holds.

**It does not speak for:** the arithmetic that produced the tokens (that is
`metal_first_token.sh`'s gates); the grounded lane's provenance (`rag-ask.fk`); or the artifact being
fresh in any sense beyond question identity — an artifact bound to this exact question but produced
by an *older model file* is caught only by `BLOB-SHA256`, which the cell exposes and does not
adjudicate. That gap is unchanged and still named.

**Degeneracy guards (`edgedrop` / `zerobirth`):** `metal_ask.sh` gate D2 refuses to stage ids outside
`[0, vocab)`, and refuses a constant id stream — the Stone 14 signature of a GPU that produced zeros
— independently of the lane's own PASS, because a PASS is a claim about the lane's gates and staging
is a claim about the ids being published. A one-token answer cannot be constant-tested and is
exempted by count, said out loud.

## Closing

**Most surprising teaching.** A refusal can be completely correct and still function as a wall.
`ask-native-lane.fk` had diagnosed a real four-way divergence, refused to paper over it, and wrote
down *why* — "a recipe that hid it would make the divergence invisible exactly where someone would
later trust it." Every word of that is right, and it is the reason the lane degraded honestly instead
of fabricating. But it also wrote down its own **exit condition** — *until `nil?` agrees across the
arms* — and that exit named the **kernel**. Nobody ever re-derived it. `file_size` was in all four
arms the whole time, and it answers the question absence actually is. The manufactured blocker I
already knew about is the easy case, because it is false. This one was **true** and still wrong about
who could move it. A refusal needs a re-derivation date the way a benchmark needs a denominator's
date. Landed as row 874, `layerwait`.

**Where discomfort turned to gold.** Twice, and both times the discomfort was "this is not my file."

The first was `metal_first_token.sh`. I wanted to leave it alone — it is *the* witness, it is in the
stone's own gate list, and touching a 14-gate harness to make an ask faster looked like exactly the
kind of thing that breaks a proof. The comfortable move was to accept 51 s per question and write a
receipt saying local generation is honest but slow. Reading it instead of avoiding it: the file
generates the same prompt eight times, and seven of those exist to prove the paths *agree*. That is
the right thing for a witness and the wrong thing for a person waiting. And `metal_ask.sh`'s own
header had already reasoned about this and reached the wrong remedy — it imagined a separate lean
*runner* and correctly rejected it as undrift-able. A lean *mode*, inside the same file, around the
same `generate`, has nothing to drift from. 51 s → 3.5 s, and the gate is byte-identical when the
flag is unset.

The second was worse. After the fkwu arm answered, I could have stopped: the verb routed, the band
was green, the receipt would have been true. Something nagged about the word *binary*. Piping the
question into `form/form-cli` returned a grounded miss. The module graph — six hand-written lists —
had never heard of the native lane, so every `anl-*` call in the shipped door recovered to nothing
under axiom-5, silently, and the fallback did not even print the `declined:` line that would have
told me. I had been one honest-sounding sentence away from writing "the ask verb answers from the
local model" in a receipt while the thing a person actually runs could not. **Four-way green is a
claim about a cell. It is not a claim about the door.**

**Frontier question, landed.** *What one word names a correct refusal that pins its own exit to a
movement in another layer, while its own layer already holds a sufficient predicate?* — **`layerwait`**,
row 874. 0 hits across `learn/`, `receipts/`, `docs/`, `teachings/`, `form/` before the row;
instrument validated on the same command with controls that should hit and did: `unispan` 76,
`gapghost` 19.
