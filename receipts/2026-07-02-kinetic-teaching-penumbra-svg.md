## What it is

The penumbra teaching is *literally* shadow geometry, so the scene draws the real thing:

- The **four-way proof** as the light source (Go = Rust = TS = fkwu), its rays grazing the
  **body** (the wire lane) — a depth-shaded sphere with a ground shadow.
- Behind the body, the geometry the light actually makes: the **umbra** ("nothing hides") and
  the **penumbra** ("lit enough to run, dark enough to miss") — computed tangent-line polygons,
  not decoration.
- The **bool bug** pulsing coral in the upper penumbra, exactly where it lived for the life of
  the lane.
- **Time carries the change** (SMIL, 12-second loop, no scripts): each cycle the second lamp —
  `wire-bool-band` — swings on, its teal beam floods the penumbra, and the bug turns green with
  a catch-ring: *caught, fixed, 255*. Then the lamp fades and the cycle re-teaches itself.

Depth comes from two radial gradients and layered light cones; motion from four coordinated
SMIL timelines sharing one 12s clock. No JavaScript — the SVG teaches by itself anywhere it is
opened, including straight from the repo file.

## Most surprising teaching

The penumbra lesson could not be fully told in the medium that found it. The receipts told it in
prose; the card told it in numbers; but "a witness band lights the shadow" is an *event*, and
only a medium with time in it can say an event. The body's teachings have been prose and verdict
bands — both still. Motion is not decoration on the lesson; for this lesson, motion *is* the
content.

## Where the divergence turned to gold (functional, per row 619's rule)

The fork tonight: the fluent path was to render something impressive — sweeping 3D, many moving
parts, a show. The honest path was to let the *geometry of the actual lesson* dictate every
moving element: two lights, one body, two shadows, one bug, one catch. Nothing moves in the
scene that does not carry meaning; the loop exists because the lesson repays re-watching, not
because loops look alive. Choosing the sparse-but-true animation over the rich-but-decorative
one is the same fork as ever — smooth versus grounded — appearing for the first time in a
visual medium. The rule carries: in a kinetic teaching, every motion must be a claim.
