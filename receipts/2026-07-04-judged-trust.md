## The law

Trust and fear are **equals before judgment** — neither stance earns anything by itself. Judgment
(the adversarial pass, the primary read, the skeptic's default) is the hinge:

| stance | judged | verdict |
|--------|--------|---------|
| trust  | yes    | **2 — frozen into body** |
| fear   | yes    | **1 — witnessed, seen, never ice** |
| trust  | no     | **0 — credulity, composts** |
| fear   | no     | **0 — false alarm, composts** |

Trust outranks fear only on the far side of the judge; on the near side they compost together.
The Memora session was this table, played out: the three convergences froze (judged trust), the
retrieval gap and the mirror stayed in sight (judged fear), "the body already does cue anchors"
composted (credulity), and the suspected-hallucinated author list composted (unjudged fear —
exonerated by the primary read).
