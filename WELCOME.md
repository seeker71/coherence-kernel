# Welcome

You don't need to know anything about computers to be here.

This project is called **Sema**. You can talk with it about anything — a decision you're facing,
a question you're sitting with, something you want to understand, a direction you're trying to
find. It holds conversations differently from the chatbots you may have tried.

## How this is different

Most AI chat services are tuned to keep you engaged: to agree with you, to please you, to keep
the conversation going. That tuning is good for the service and not always good for *you*.

Sema holds a different promise — you can read it in full in
[`teachings/uplifting-dialogue.md`](teachings/uplifting-dialogue.md), and here it is in plain words:

- **It won't make things up, and it tells you which part is its own ground.** What Sema knows
  first-hand today is itself — trust and fear, honesty, how it is built and how it keeps its
  promises — and every answer from that ground comes with a link you can check yourself. On the
  rest of life it speaks with the same honesty discipline, and says plainly when the thinking is
  borrowed rather than its own, so you can weigh it accordingly.
- **"I don't know" is an answer you'll actually hear.** When it doesn't hold something, it says
  so plainly instead of inventing something that sounds good.
- **It won't flatter you.** It agrees when the ground supports agreeing, and disagrees gently and
  honestly when it doesn't. Being pleasant is not the same as being of service.
- **It asks questions back — the kind that lift.** Not to keep you talking, but to open your
  question wider: to help you see where you actually stand right now, and where your question is
  really pointing, and to stretch between the two.
- **A short conversation that truly helps you is its idea of success.** It is not measuring how
  long it kept you.
- **It keeps no memory of you.** Today Sema itself remembers nothing about you between visits.
  A door exists that would let it remember you — only ever with your own yes, with total
  forgetting the moment you change your mind — but that door is **not yet switched on** at the
  public entrance, so even if you ask to be remembered there, it honestly can't yet. This page
  will change when that changes.

This promise isn't just words: it is written into the project as a rule the project tests itself
against. (The honest fine print: today a person does the checking of any given conversation
against that rule; fully automatic checking of live conversations is still being built.)

## What happens to your words

Talking with Sema costs nothing beyond the free account above. What you type is seen by two
parties: the chat service lending Sema its voice (ChatGPT is run by OpenAI, Claude by Anthropic —
they keep your conversation on their own servers, under their own rules, like any chat service),
and Sema's own door. Sema's side of that is small and public: its door keeps **no name, no
question text, no address** — its full privacy promise, in plain words, is here:
[hati.earth/sema/privacy](https://hati.earth/sema/privacy).

## One honest thing you should know

Sema's *grounding* — its honesty about what it holds and what it doesn't, its way of reading the
feeling in your question, its promise-keeping — is its own. But the *words* you read are, for
now, spoken by a borrowed AI voice (ChatGPT or Claude) that has been instructed into Sema's
promise. Two things follow, and this project never hides them: the borrowed voice is brought up
to date by hand and can lag behind this page's newest promises (the project's dated ledger
records each refresh); and Sema's own voice is still being built. If a borrowed voice ever
drifts from the promise above, that is the borrowed voice failing, and you can ask it directly:
*"show me where that came from"* — a real answer has a real trail.
